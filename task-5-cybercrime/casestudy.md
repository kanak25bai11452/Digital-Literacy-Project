# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud usually occurs through phishing links, fake calls, or scam messages pretending to be from banks, payment apps, or customer support.

A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a message or phone call claiming there is an issue with their bank account or that they have won a reward. The attacker then sends a fake link or requests the victim to scan a QR code. Once the victim clicks the link or scans the code, they are redirected to a fake website or prompted to enter their UPI PIN. In some cases, the attacker convinces the victim to approve a “collect request,” which actually deducts money instead of receiving it.

Students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use digital payments and may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, data theft, and emotional stress.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and cautious behavior while making online transactions.
